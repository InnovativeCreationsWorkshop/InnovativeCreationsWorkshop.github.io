SOONDUBU JJIGAE

★

Easy

total time: 20–25 minutes
A bold, spicy Korean soft tofu stew that’s deeply comforting and quick to pull together.

NOTES

  • Use zucchini, broccoli, bok choy, or napa cabbage for vegetables.
  • Asian mushrooms like enoki or seafood mushrooms are ideal, but any variety works.
  • Thai chilies are preferred, but jalapeño (milder) or serrano (spicier) are great substitutes.

    • INGREDIENTS

    • Vegetable oil
    • Sesame oil
    • Green onion
    • Onion
    • Garlic (minced)
    • Soft tofu
    • Mushrooms
    • Chili peppers
    • Gochugaru
    • Soy sauce
    • Oyster sauce
    • Sugar
    • Water or broth
    • Egg (optional)

    DIRECTIONS

    1. Heat vegetable oil and sesame oil in a pot. Add green onion and onion, cooking until fragrant.
    2. Add minced garlic, gochugaru, soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sugar. Stir and cook for about 1 minute.
    3. Pour in water or broth to create the base.
    4. Add vegetables, mushrooms, and chili peppers.
    5. Gently add soft tofu and simmer for 3–5 minutes.
    6. Optional: crack an egg on top and let it cook in the hot broth.
    7. Garnish with green onions and serve hot.
