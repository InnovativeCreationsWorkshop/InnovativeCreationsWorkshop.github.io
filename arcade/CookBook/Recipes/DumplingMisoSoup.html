DUMPLING MISO SOUP

★

Easy

total time: 20–25 minutes
A comforting bowl of savory miso broth loaded with dumplings, mushrooms, and fresh vegetables. Perfect for chilly days or a quick weeknight dinner.

NOTES

  • Add cabbage, leeks, bok choy, or any vegetables you have on hand.
  • Bone broth creates a richer flavor, and dried mushrooms or umami seasoning deepen the broth even more.
  • Top with green onions and a drizzle of sriracha for extra flavor and heat.

    • INGREDIENTS

    • Sesame oil
    • Small yellow onion, diced
    • Fresh ginger, grated
    • Garlic, minced
    • Soy sauce
    • Mushrooms, sliced
    • Celery, chopped
    • Hot water or bone broth
    • Miso paste
    • Frozen dumplings
    • Green onions, sliced
    • Sriracha (optional)

    DIRECTIONS

    1. Heat sesame oil in a pot over medium heat. Sauté the onion, ginger, and garlic until fragrant and softened.
    2. Stir in the soy sauce, then add the mushrooms and celery. Cook for 3–4 minutes.
    3. Pour in hot water or bone broth to your desired amount. Add dried mushrooms or umami seasoning if using.
    4. Whisk in the miso paste until fully dissolved.
    5. Add the dumplings and simmer until they are cooked through according to the package directions.
    6. Serve topped with sliced green onions and sriracha if desired.
