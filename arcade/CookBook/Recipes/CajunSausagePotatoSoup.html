CAJUN SAUSAGE POTATO SOUP

★

Easy

total time: 45–55 minutes
Smoky and creamy with Cajun heat, crispy bacon, and fork-tender potatoes. Basically sweater weather in a bowl.

NOTES

  • For more spice, swap bell pepper for jalapeño or another hot pepper.
  • Mash the potatoes slightly before serving for a thicker, creamier soup.
  • Top with parmesan and scallions for garnish.


    • INGREDIENTS

    • Bacon, diced
    • Andouille sausage, sliced
    • Bell pepper, diced
    • Onion, diced
    • Celery, diced
    • Garlic, diced
    • Butter
    • Onion powder
    • Garlic powder
    • Cajun seasoning (Slap Ya Mama)
    • Italian seasoning
    • Flour
    • Bone broth
    • Water
    • Potatoes, diced
    • Heavy cream
    • Parmesan and scallions for garnish

    DIRECTIONS

    1. In a large pot, cook diced bacon until crispy. Remove and set aside.
    2. Cook the andouille sausage in the bacon grease until browned. Remove and set aside.
    3. Using the grease from the meats, cook the bell pepper, onion, celery, and garlic until softened.
    4. Add butter and cook for a few minutes.
    5. Season with onion powder, garlic powder, Cajun seasoning, and Italian seasoning.
    6. Sprinkle in flour and stir to create a roux.
    7. Slowly add bone broth and water while stirring.
    8. Add diced potatoes and simmer until fork tender.
    9. Pour in heavy cream, then return the bacon and sausage to the pot.
    10. Cook another 5–10 minutes, garnish, and serve hot.
