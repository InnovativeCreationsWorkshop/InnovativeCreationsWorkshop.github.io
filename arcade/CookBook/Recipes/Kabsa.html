KABSA

★ ★ ★ ★

Medium-Hard

total time: 1.5–2 hours
Fragrant Gulf-style spiced rice layered with citrusy chicken, deep aromatics, and fresh daqoos sauce.

NOTES

  • The layering of flavors is what makes kabsa special — take your time with the spice base.
  • Loomi (dried black lime) gives the dish its signature earthy citrus flavor.
  • Broiling the chicken after simmering creates the classic golden crispy finish.
  • For an easier version, cook the rice in a rice cooker using the seasoned stock.

    DIRECTIONS

    1. Heat ghee or butter in a large pot.
    2. Add diced onion and grated carrot. Cook until softened.
    3. Add cinnamon sticks, bay leaves, cardamom pods, cloves, star anise, and loomi.
    4. Add black pepper, garlic powder, ginger powder, paprika, chili powder, coriander powder, nutmeg, cinnamon powder, cardamom powder, cumin, turmeric, and loomi powder.
    5. Mix in tomato puree, chicken bouillon, and salt.
    6. Cook everything down until it forms a rich fragrant paste.
    7. Add chicken and fry until lightly colored.
    8. Add water and orange zest, then simmer for about 30 minutes or until chicken is fully cooked.
    9. Remove chicken and broil until crispy and golden.
    10. Wash and soak basmati rice until doubled slightly in size.
    11. Add rice to the remaining seasoned stock and cook until fluffy and tender.
    12. Add extra orange zest for brightness before serving.
    13. Blend or finely chop all daqoos ingredients together and serve alongside the kabsa.
