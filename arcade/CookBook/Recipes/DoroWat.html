DORO WAT

★ ★ ★

Advanced

total time: 2–2.5 hours
Deeply spiced Ethiopian chicken stew with slow-cooked onions, rich berbere heat, and eggs soaked in flavorful sauce.

NOTES

  • The onions are the foundation of the stew — use an excessive amount and cook them patiently.
  • Berbere is powerful and spicy, so add gradually and taste as you go.
  • Korerima can be substituted with cardamom if unavailable.
  • Cutting shallow slits into the eggs helps them absorb the sauce.


    • INGREDIENTS

    • Chicken legs or wings
    • Lemon juice
    • Hardboiled eggs
    • Red onions or shallots (large amount)
    • Berbere spice
    • Tomato paste
    • Korerima (or cardamom)
    • Kibe (or butter)
    • Maklesha spice blend
    • Water, as needed
    • Salt, to taste

    DIRECTIONS

    1. Boil eggs until hardboiled, then set aside to cool.
    2. Wash chicken and marinate with lemon juice while preparing the stew base.
    3. Finely chop a very large amount of onions or shallots.
    4. Cook onions on low to medium heat for a long time until deeply caramelized.
    5. Add berbere spice carefully, stirring constantly so it does not burn.
    6. Mix in tomato paste and korerima.
    7. Add water as needed to keep the stew from drying out.
    8. Add chicken to the stew and cook until tender and fully done.
    9. Stir in kibe and 1 tablespoon of maklesha spice blend.
    10. Peel eggs and make shallow slits around each egg.
    11. Add eggs to the stew and simmer so they absorb the flavor.
    12. Serve hot with injera, roti, or string hoppers.
