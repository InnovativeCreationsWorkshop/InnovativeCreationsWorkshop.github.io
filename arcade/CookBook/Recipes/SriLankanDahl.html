SRI LANKAN DAHL

★

Easy

total time: 30–40 minutes
Comforting lentils simmered with warm spices and coconut milk. Creamy, fragrant, and built for rice.

NOTES

  • Drain the lentils for a thicker texture, or leave some liquid for a smoother, softer dahl.
  • Adjust simmering time depending on how creamy or chunky you want the final result.
  • Works beautifully as either a main dish or a side.

    • INGREDIENTS

    • Red lentils
    • Salt
    • Turmeric
    • Onion, sliced
    • Mustard seeds
    • Dahl masala
    • Coconut milk
    • Rice, for serving

    DIRECTIONS

    1. Wash the red lentils thoroughly until the water runs mostly clear.
    2. Boil the lentils like rice. Halfway through cooking, add salt and turmeric.
    3. Cook until just al dente, then remove from heat and drain if you prefer a thicker dahl. Leave some liquid for a smoother texture.
    4. In a pan, fry onion and mustard seeds until aromatic and lightly softened.
    5. Add more turmeric, salt, and dahl masala. Stir until the spices form a fragrant paste.
    6. Add the cooked lentils into the pan and cook for 5–10 minutes depending on the texture you want.
    7. Pour in the coconut milk, mix well, and simmer until creamy and fully combined.
    8. Serve hot with rice as a main dish or side.
