TOFU POT

★

Easy

total time: 25–30 minutes
Light, savory, and comforting. Crispy tofu meets soft cabbage in a simple, slurpable broth.

NOTES

  • Add carrots, bok choy, spinach, broccoli, or mushrooms for extra vegetables.
  • Swap in sundubu-jjigae sauce for a deeper, richer flavor.
  • Use jalapeño, Thai chilies, or any chilies you have on hand.
  • No fresh chilies? Substitute with sriracha or chili paste.
  • Garnish with green onions for a fresh finish.
  • Cook until the cabbage is tender for the best texture.

    • INGREDIENTS

    • Firm tofu (cut into triangles)
    • Salt, black pepper
    • Eggs (beaten smooth)
    • Napa cabbage (chopped or sliced like noodles)
    • Bone broth

    SOUP BASE

    • Water
    • Dark soy sauce
    • Light soy sauce
    • Rice vinegar
    • Oyster sauce
    • Garlic (minced)
    • Asian/Thai chilies

    DIRECTIONS

    1. Season tofu with salt and pepper, then cut into triangles.
    2. Dip tofu pieces into beaten eggs, coating evenly.
    3. Pan-fry tofu until golden on both sides, then remove from pan.
    4. In a pot, add napa cabbage along with bone broth, tofu and sauce
    5. Simmer until cabbage is soft and flavors are combined.
