BEEF STEW

★★★

Medium Hard

total time: 2.5–3 hours
A rich, slow-simmered stew with tender beef, vegetables, and deep savory flavor.

NOTES

  • Feeling lazy, use it in a crockpot, on high for 5 hours.
  • Red wine is optional but adds depth to the broth.
  • Finish with fresh parsley for brightness.

    • INGREDIENTS

    • Chuck roast (cubed)
    • Salt
    • Black pepper
    • Paprika
    • Onion powder
    • Garlic powder
    • Flour
    • Onion
    • Celery
    • Garlic
    • Tomato paste
    • Beef broth
    • Water
    • Red wine (optional)
    • Bay leaf
    • Fresh thyme
    • Fresh rosemary
    • Potatoes
    • Carrots
    • Parsley (for garnish)

    DIRECTIONS

    1. Cube chuck roast and season with salt, black pepper, paprika, onion powder, and garlic powder. Toss with flour until evenly coated.
    2. In a large pot, brown the beef in batches until well seared, then remove and set aside.
    3. In the same pot, add chopped onion, celery, and garlic. Cook until softened.
    4. Stir in tomato paste and cook for 1–2 minutes.
    5. Add broth, water, and optional red wine, scraping up browned bits from the bottom.
    6. Return beef to the pot. Add bay leaf, thyme, and rosemary.
    7. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 1 hour 30 minutes.
    8. Add chopped potatoes and carrots, then simmer another 30–40 minutes until beef is very tender.
    9. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.
