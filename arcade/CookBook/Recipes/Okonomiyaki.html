OKONOMIYAKI

★★

Medium

total time: 25–35 minutes
Part pancake, part cabbage engineering project, completely delicious.

NOTES

  • Use napa cabbage or regular cabbage sliced thin like noodles.
  • Customize freely with shrimp, bacon, squid, or any protein you like.
  • Store-bought okonomiyaki sauce works great, but homemade is easy too.
  • Top with kewpie mayo, bonito flakes, seaweed, or whatever sounds good.

INGREDIENTS

  • Shredded cabbage
  • Spring onion
  • Egg
  • Flour
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Oil for frying
  • Worcestershire sauce
  • Ketchup
  • Sugar
  • Oyster sauce
  • Kewpie mayo
  • Bonito flakes
  • Optional proteins/toppings

DIRECTIONS

  1. Shred cabbage thinly and combine with spring onion.
  2. Add salt, pepper, egg, flour, and water to form a thick batter.
  3. Mix until fully combined.
  4. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat.
  5. Pour mixture into pan and shape into a round pancake.
  6. Cook until golden brown, then flip and cook the other side.
  7. Mix Worcestershire sauce, ketchup, sugar, and oyster sauce for topping.
  8. Finish with sauce, kewpie mayo, bonito flakes, and toppings.
