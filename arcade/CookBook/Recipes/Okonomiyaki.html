OKONOMIYAKI

★★

Medium

total time: 25–35 minutes
Part pancake, part cabbage engineering project, completely delicious.

NOTES

  • Use napa cabbage or regular cabbage sliced thin like noodles.
  • Customize freely with shrimp, bacon, squid, or any protein you like.
  • Store-bought okonomiyaki sauce works great, but homemade is easy too.
  • Top with kewpie mayo, bonito flakes, seaweed, or whatever sounds good.

    • INGREDIENTS

    • Shredded cabbage
    • Spring onion
    • Salt
    • Black pepper
    • Egg
    • Flour
    • Water
    • Oil for frying
    • Worcestershire sauce
    • Ketchup
    • Sugar
    • Oyster sauce
    • Kewpie mayo
    • Bonito flakes
    • Optional toppings and proteins

    DIRECTIONS

    1. Shred the cabbage thinly and place it into a bowl with sliced spring onion.
    2. Add salt, pepper, egg, flour, and enough water to create a thick batter.
    3. Mix everything well until fully combined.
    4. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat.
    5. Add the mixture and shape it into a large pancake.
    6. Cook until golden brown on the bottom, then carefully flip and cook the other side.
    7. For the sauce, mix Worcestershire sauce, ketchup, sugar, and oyster sauce.
    8. Top the okonomiyaki with sauce, kewpie mayo, bonito flakes, and any toppings you like.
