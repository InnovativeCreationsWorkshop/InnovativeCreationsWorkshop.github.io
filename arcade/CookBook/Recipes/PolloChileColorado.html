POLLO EN CHILE COLORADO

★★

Medium

total time: 35–45 minutes
Tender chicken simmered in a smoky red chile sauce that tastes like effort, even if you took shortcuts.

NOTES

  • Serve with Spanish rice and beans of your choice. Pinto or black beans work best.
  • Using boxed rice is completely acceptable lazy-mode behavior.
  • Optional toppings: Mexican cheese blend, queso fresco, or cotija cheese.
  • If extremely lazy, substitute homemade sauce with canned red enchilada sauce.

    • INGREDIENTS

    • Cube Chicken
    • Salt
    • Black pepper
    • Onion powder
    • Garlic powder
    • 15 guajillo chiles
    • 1 ancho chile
    • 7 chile de árbol peppers
    • 1 tomato
    • 3 garlic cloves
    • 2 cups water
    • Oregano
    • Cumin
    • Knorr chicken bouillon
    • Cilantro
    • Cheese of choice

    DIRECTIONS

    1. Cube the chicken and season with salt, pepper, onion powder, and garlic powder.
    2. Cook chicken in a pan over medium-high heat until browned on the outside.
    3. In a pot, boil the guajillo chiles, ancho chile, chile de árbol peppers, tomato, and garlic until softened.
    4. Add the boiled ingredients to a blender or food processor with 2 cups of water, salt, oregano, cumin, and Knorr chicken bouillon.
    5. Blend until completely smooth.
    6. Strain the sauce directly into the pan with the chicken.
    7. Simmer until the chicken is fully cooked and the sauce thickens slightly.
    8. Top with cheese and fresh cilantro before serving.
