THAI CHICKEN SATAY

★★

Medium

total time: 1 hour 15 minutes
Tender marinated chicken skewers with bold Thai-inspired flavors, charred edges, and a creamy peanut sauce finish. Perfect with an Asian-style salad or crunchy slaw.

NOTES

  • Flattening the chicken with a mallet helps it cook evenly and stay tender.
  • Add cayenne pepper if you prefer extra heat.
  • Chicken can be cubed or sliced into thin strips before marinating.
  • Serve with peanut sauce and an Asian-inspired salad or slaw.


    • INGREDIENTS

    • Chicken breast or thighs
    • Curry powder
    • Soy sauce
    • Fish sauce
    • Thai red curry paste
    • Pinch of sugar
    • Cayenne pepper (optional)
    • Peanut sauce, for serving
    • Asian-style salad or slaw, for serving
    • Wooden or metal skewers

    DIRECTIONS

    1. Cut chicken into cubes or thin tender-sized pieces. If using thin pieces, flatten lightly with a mallet.
    2. In a bowl, combine curry powder, soy sauce, fish sauce, Thai red curry paste, sugar, and cayenne if using.
    3. Add chicken and toss until fully coated. Marinate for at least 30 minutes.
    4. Thread the chicken onto skewers.
    5. Heat a skillet over high heat and sear the skewers until nicely browned on all sides.
    6. If using thicker pieces, transfer to a 375°F (190°C) oven and bake for 20 minutes.
    7. Switch the oven to broil and cook for about 10 minutes, rotating the skewers to color each side evenly.
    8. Serve hot with peanut sauce and an Asian-inspired salad or slaw.
