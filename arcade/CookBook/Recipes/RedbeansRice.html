RED BEANS & RICE

★★★

Medium Hard

total time: ~1.5–2 hours
This classic Cajun southern comfort dish brings together slow-simmered red beans, smoky spices, and tender rice for a hearty, soulful meal.

NOTES

  • Garnish with green onions and dried parsley.
  • If using dried beans, soak them overnight (this is a crucial step). If using canned beans, cook time will be much shorter.
  • You can substitute bell pepper for poblano pepper if needed.
  • Use Slap Ya Mama seasoning instead of salt.
  • For a crockpot version, cook on high for about 4 hours.
  • Optional: add a turkey leg if you want extra richness and flavor.

    • INGREDIENTS

    • Rice
    • Sausage
    • Holy Trinity
      (Celery, Bell Peppers, onion)
    • Garlic
    • Butter
    • Red beans
    • Bone broth
    • Bay leaf
    • Flour
    • Salt
    • Black pepper
    • Garlic powder
    • Onion powder
    • Oregano
    • Thyme
    • Paprika
    • Cayenne pepper
    • Red chili flakes
    • Chicken bouillon

    DIRECTIONS

    1. Brown the sausage in a large pot, then remove and set aside.
    2. Sauté onion, garlic, in butter until soft.
    3. Add the rest of the Holy Trinity (Celery, Peppers).
    4. Stir in flour and cook for a minute.
    5. Add all spices and mix well.
    6. Pour in bone broth and add the bay leaf. Bring to a boil.
    7. Return sausage and add red beans, then reduce to a low medium heat for 1-1.5 hours, covered, until beans tender.
    8. Adjust seasoning to taste.
    9. Serve over rice and garnish with green onions and parsley.
