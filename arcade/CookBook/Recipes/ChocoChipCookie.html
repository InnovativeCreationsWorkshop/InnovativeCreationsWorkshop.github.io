THE BEST CHOCOLATE CHIP COOKIES

★★

Medium

total time: 30–35 minutes
Brown butter cookies with crisp edges, gooey centers, and pools of melted chocolate in every bite.

NOTES

  • Freezing extra dough balls makes future cookie emergencies very manageable.
  • Using both chopped chocolate chunks and semi-sweet chips creates the perfect mix of melty pockets and classic cookie texture.

    • INGREDIENTS

    • 1 block butter
    • 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 tsp salt
    • 1 tsp baking soda
    • 1 cup brown sugar
    • 1/2 cup white sugar
    • 1 tsp vanilla extract
    • 1 egg
    • 1 egg yolk
    • Chopped chocolate chunks
    • Semi-sweet chocolate chips

    DIRECTIONS

    1. Brown the butter in a pan over medium heat until golden and nutty smelling. Let it cool slightly.
    2. In one bowl, whisk together the flour, salt, and baking soda.
    3. In a separate bowl, combine brown sugar, white sugar, vanilla, egg, and egg yolk. Mix until smooth.
    4. Pour in the cooled browned butter and mix well.
    5. Add the dry ingredients into the wet mixture and stir until just combined.
    6. Fold in chopped chocolate chunks and semi-sweet chocolate chips.
    7. Roll the dough into balls. Freeze extra dough for later baking if desired.
    8. Bake at 350°F for about 14 minutes until the edges are crisp and the centers stay soft and gooey.
