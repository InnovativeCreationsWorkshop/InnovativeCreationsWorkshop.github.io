INDIAN BEEF VINDALOO

★★★

Medium Hard

total time: 2–2.5 hours
Bold, spicy, and tangy. A rich Indian curry with deep layers of heat and aromatics.

NOTES

  • Marinate overnight for deeper flavor and more tender beef.
  • Adjust the number of chilies to control heat level.
  • Serve with basmati or jasmine rice to balance the spice.

    • VINDALOO MASALA PASTE

    • Garlic cloves
    • Ginger
    • Kashmiri chilies (soaked)
    • Cinnamon
    • Cloves
    • Cardamom
    • Black peppercorns
    • Coriander seeds
    • Cumin seeds
    • Fennel seeds
    • Water

    INGREDIENTS

    • Beef chuck (cubed)
    • Salt, Black pepper
    • Vindaloo Masala Paste
    • Turmeric
    • Vinegar
    • Coconut oil
    • Mustard seeds
    • Onion
    • Curry leaves
    • Bone broth
    • Water
    • Cilantro (for garnish)
    • Rice (for serving)

    DIRECTIONS

    1. Marinate cubed beef with turmeric, salt, pepper, and vinegar for at least 30 minutes (overnight preferred).
    2. Make the vindaloo paste by blending garlic, ginger, soaked Kashmiri chilies, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, black peppercorns, coriander seeds, cumin, and fennel with a little water until smooth.
    3. Heat coconut oil in a pot until hot. Add mustard seeds, then onions and curry leaves. Cook until fragrant.
    4. Add the masala paste and cook for 2–3 minutes on medium-high heat until combined and aromatic.
    5. Add the marinated beef.
    6. Pour in bone broth and a bit of hot water to cover. Bring to a simmer.
    7. Cover and cook for about 1.5 hours, or until the beef is tender.
    8. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with basmati or jasmine rice.
