MILLE-FEUILLE NABE SOUP

★★

Medium

total time: 40–50 minutes
Layered napa cabbage and thin beef simmered in a light umami-rich dashi broth with mushrooms and warming ginger.

NOTES

  • Enoki mushrooms work best for the classic hot pot texture, but shiitake or oyster mushrooms also work well.
  • The dipping sauce adds brightness and heat that balances the rich broth beautifully.

    • INGREDIENTS

    • Napa cabbage
    • Thinly sliced beef
    • Enoki mushrooms
    • Dried kelp (kombu)
    • Bonito flakes
    • Water
    • Dashi powder (optional)
    • Soy sauce
    • Mirin
    • Chili pepper
    • Fresh ginger
    • Lemon juice
    • Scallions
    • Chili powder

    DIRECTIONS

    1. Layer napa cabbage and thinly sliced beef alternately until stacked. Cut the stack into thirds.
    2. Arrange the layered sections upright around a pot. Place enoki mushrooms in the center.
    3. Make the dashi broth by simmering dried kelp and water, then adding bonito flakes. Strain the broth, or use dashi powder for a quicker version.
    4. Season the broth with soy sauce, mirin, chili pepper, and fresh ginger.
    5. Pour the broth into the pot and bring to a gentle boil.
    6. Cook until the beef is fully cooked and the napa cabbage is tender.
    7. For the optional dipping sauce, combine soy sauce, lemon juice, scallions, and chili powder.
    8. Serve hot with the dipping sauce on the side if desired.
