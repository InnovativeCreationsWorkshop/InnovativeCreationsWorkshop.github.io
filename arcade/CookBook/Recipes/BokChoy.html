BOK CHOY WITH GARLIC SAUCE

★

Easy

total time: 15–20 minutes
Blanched greens topped with hot garlic sauce that wakes everything up instantly.

NOTES

  • Pairs well with KBBQ steak, teriyaki chicken, or braised tofu.
  • Blanching time is important—overcooking will make the greens lose their bite and bright color.
  • Add chili flakes or gochugaru for extra heat.
  • Vegetable of Choice: Cabbage, spinach, broccoli.
  • Add shredded carrots and green onions to turn it into more of a salad.

    • INGREDIENTS

    • Bok choy
    • Vegetable of Choice (optional)
    • Oyster sauce
    • Soy sauce
    • Garlic (minced)
    • Sesame oil
    • Pinch of sugar
    • Protein of choice

    DIRECTIONS

    1. Bring water to a boil and blanch bok choy and any optional greens until just tender and bright.
    2. Drain immediately and set aside.
    3. In a small pan, combine oyster sauce, soy sauce, minced garlic, sesame oil, and a pinch of sugar.
    4. Heat the sauce over medium heat for 1–2 minutes until fragrant and glossy, without over-reducing.
    5. Arrange blanched greens in a bowl or plate.
    6. Drizzle hot garlic sauce generously over the greens.
    7. East as is or serve with your choice of BBQ steak, teriyaki chicken, or braised tofu on top or on the side.
