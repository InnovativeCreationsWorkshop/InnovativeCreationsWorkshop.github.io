GlowAR:
An Interactive Wall Painting

Technology Used: TouchDesigner, Microsoft Kinect

Art and technology collide in this immersive augmented reality drawing experience, where participants use glow sticks to create luminous, real-time digital paintings. Evolving from previous Interactive Drawing projects, this piece reimagines artistic expression by leveraging the Microsoft Kinect and Touch Designer to transform movement into dynamic visual compositions. Unlike earlier iterations that relied on skeletal tracking or depth mapping, this version captures live color data, allowing for a more fluid and spontaneous artistic interaction.

At the core of this piece, a custom-built system in Touch Designer isolates and amplifies the brightness of certain color pixels using GLSL nodes and Python scripting. This technique generates a mesmerizing feedback loop, creating the effect of glowing brushstrokes in a digital space. By using glow sticks, the system effectively detects and translates movement into vibrant trails of light, offering participants complete creative freedom.

During the three-day exhibition, visitors stepped into the interactive installation, using glow sticks to craft ephemeral artworks in real time. This unique experience not only blurred the boundaries between the physical and digital worlds but also fostered a sense of community and dialogueâ€”each participant contributing to a continuously evolving collaborative masterpiece. The project transcended traditional artistic mediums, highlighting the power of technology to create new forms of shared human expression.

Interactive Light Experience

This project creates an immersive experience where users interact with light traces generated by their smartphone screens. Using a webcam to capture the light, the system transforms it into glowing lines that evolve into particles as they move. These particles are influenced by the surrounding music, with audio inputs triggering dynamic changes in the particle system. The result is a fluid, audio-reactive visual experience where the movement of light and the rhythm of the music create an ever-changing, atmospheric composition.

Conclusion

The thesis showcase featured a collection of curated works that represented the final iterations of a daily sketch practice. Included in the showcase were an interactive wall, an interactive hologram, and an augmented/virtual reality experience. Throughout the show, both individuals and groups engaged with the artwork, generating emotional responses, particularly a sense of joy. The showcase was designed to demonstrate that art objects, when paired with technology, can transcend traditional forms and offer a deeper engagement with the viewer. By presenting interactive works in a public space, the experience invited viewers to actively participate, aligning with the principles of the dialogic aesthetic, where the audience becomes an integral part of the art itself.
