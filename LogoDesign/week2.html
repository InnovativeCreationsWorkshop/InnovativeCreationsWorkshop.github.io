Week 2: Principles of Design and Creative Process

Presentation

Assignment

Research

  • Study the Market/ Case studies
    Who else is engaging in activities similar to your brand? What strategies are they employing that are yielding success? Conversely, what approaches are proving unsuccessful for them? Who comprises their target demographic? And finally, who are your direct competitors in the market?
  • Brainstorm/Word Dump
    Create a list of adjectives that evoke desired emotions and visuals to be associated with your brand/logo
  • Moodboard
    Generate a collage using images inspired by your brainstorming session. The aim is to identify colors, shapes, themes, and designs that you envision incorporating into your logo.

Ideation

  • Reference
    Let your moodboard inform your search for references as you design your logo.
  • Quick Sketch
    Quick sketch 20-50 logos, with by notes. Remember, the most innovative ideas and designs often originate from doodles.

Concept

  • Logo Selection
    From your selection of logo designs, choose a couple that strongly resonate with your brand and its identity.
  • Polish
    Using Photoshop, Photopea, Procreate, or a piece of paper polish the selected logos.
  • Final Selection
    Finally select the one that works the best, copy or scan it trace it in Illustrator

Brand Brief

Consider the Following:

  • What is the brand's core purpose or mission?
    Understanding why your brand exists beyond making profits is crucial. Ask yourself what drives your brand and what impact you aim to make on your audience or the world.
  • Who is the target audience?
    Define your ideal customers in detail. What are their demographics, psychographics, preferences, and pain points? Knowing your audience helps tailor your brand identity to appeal to them effectively.
  • What are the brand's values and personality traits?
    Identify the characteristics that define your brand's personality. Is it adventurous, trustworthy, sophisticated, or playful? Align these traits with your target audience's preferences and your brand's mission.
  • How does the brand differentiate itself from competitors?
    Analyze your competitors' brand identities and determine what sets your brand apart. What unique value proposition or selling points do you offer? Highlighting these distinctions will help create a memorable brand identity.
  • What visual elements represent the brand effectively?
    Consider the visual aspects of your brand, such as colors, typography, imagery, and logo design. How do these elements convey your brand's values, personality, and differentiation? Ensure consistency across all visual components to reinforce your brand identity effectively
